Section 01
The Question This Walkthrough Answers
Not "what does the screen look like" — "what has to be true for this to work"

This partnership makes one specific claim: a product can be made exclusively available through Rakuten, and Rakuten can prove — not just assert — that it was the reason the sale happened. Everything in this walkthrough exists to make that claim concrete, scenario by scenario.

What has to be trueWhere it's tested in the journey
The offer has to feel like it was selected for this member, not a generic coupon with an "exclusive" sticker slapped onScenario 1 — discovery & offer detail
The member has to trust the handoff from Rakuten's site to a merchant's site enough to actually buy — and the product genuinely has to be unreachable any other wayScenario 1 — redirect & merchant landing
Rakuten has to be able to show, not tell, that it gets credit for the saleScenario 1 — confirmation & attribution proof
The same mechanic has to answer a different, specific merchant business question depending on who's targetedScenarios 2 & 3 — Early Access and Lapsed Re-engagement

Section 05
One Flow, Three Reasons to Use It
The same mechanics, three different business justifications for why Rakuten should send this offer at all

Everything above describes the mechanical journey — offer, handoff, purchase, proof. But the same exclusivity mechanic is only valuable to a merchant if it's aimed at a member for a reason the merchant actually cares about. The same screens play out differently depending on why Rakuten decided this member should see the offer:

ScenarioWho's being targetedThe merchant's actual question this answers
Exclusive DropAn engaged member with category affinity — no prior relationship required"Can you get me buyers I couldn't reach myself?"
Early Access WindowA newly acquired member, given first access as a welcome moment"Can you make a new customer's first experience feel special?"
Lapsed Re-engagementA member who bought before and went quiet 90+ days"Can you bring back someone I've already lost?" — the sale that provably wouldn't have happened otherwise
Why Lapsed Re-engagement is worth calling out specifically There's no ambiguity about whether the sale was already going to happen. A member who bought nothing in 90+ days, then buys the day after receiving this offer, is about as clean a causal story as a merchant is ever going to get.
